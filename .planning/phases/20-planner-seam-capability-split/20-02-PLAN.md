<objective>
Implement the per-connection capability split (PLANNER-02) and the accept-loop extension that
lets exactly one planner-role connection join a session without ever holding a mint verb or an
fd (PLANNER-04). This is the security-critical TCB change of the phase: it must close the
planner off from all four trust-minting verbs and from raw-bytes access, fail-closed and
default-deny, WITHOUT disturbing Phase 19's one-way occupancy latch or the single-worker model.

Purpose: Phase 21 will connect a real (adversarial) LLM planner to the broker on a second,
capability-restricted connection. That connection must be structurally incapable of minting a
`UserTrusted` handle or reading untrusted bytes ‚Äî the tainted-vs-trusted determination stays
entirely with the worker's mint sites (DESIGN ¬ß3/¬ß5). This plan builds and proves that seam.

Output: A `ConnectionRole`/capability model, a `DeclarePlannerRole` establishment handshake,
the extended accept loop, the pre-dispatch capability gate, and the tests proving both the
new rejections and Phase 19's unmodified regression pass.
</objective>

<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| planner-role connection ‚Üí broker mint sites | Untrusted (an adversarial planner, Phase 21). Must be structurally unable to mint a `UserTrusted` handle or reach `quarantine.rs`. |
| planner-role connection ‚Üí workspace fd | Must never cross ‚Äî planner gets no `RequestFd`, so no raw untrusted bytes. |
| 2nd connection ‚Üí session occupancy | A 2nd connection is the ¬ß2-forbidden case unless it is the single declared planner connection. |

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Severity | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-20-01 | Elevation of Privilege | mint verbs on a planner connection (`ProvideIntent`/`ReportClaims`/`ReportDerivedClaim`/`CreateSession`) | critical | mitigate | `ConnectionRole::permits` denies all four for `Planner`; the pre-dispatch gate in `handle_connection` returns fail-closed `Error` before `dispatch_request`, minting nothing. | T-20-02 | Spoofing | role re-declaration mid-stream to acquire mint verbs | high | mitigate | Role is fixed once at establishment (first frame) and threaded immutably; `DeclarePlannerRole` arriving mid-stream is itself a non-permitted verb for a planner connection (denied), and a worker connection never becomes a planner. No per-message role re-derivation. |
