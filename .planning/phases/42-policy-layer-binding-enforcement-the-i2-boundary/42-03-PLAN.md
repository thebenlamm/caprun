<objective>
Wire the policy layer into the executor as a PRE-I2 narrowing gate per DESIGN §5.1/§5.2, and prove POLICY-02 (the LOCKED invariant) with an enforcement-order test. `executor::submit_plan_node` gains a `policy: &SessionPolicy` parameter; immediately after the Step-0 schema gate and BEFORE the collect-then-Block I2 loop, a deny-only gate evaluates the policy — a non-permitted sink or arg returns `Denied { reason: DenyReason::PolicyDeny{..} }`; a PERMIT does nothing (falls through) so the UNMODIFIED I2 loop still runs on every permitted call. The policy param is threaded through `brokerd::submit_plan_node` and `server.rs::evaluate_plan_node_and_record`, with `run_broker_server` constructing the policy value internally for now (Plan 04 replaces that with a trusted-source-bound policy).

Purpose: POLICY-01 needs a distinct policy-deny outcome that is separate from an I2 Block; POLICY-02 needs I2 to execute unconditionally on every policy-permitted call, never short-circuited by any policy outcome. The load-bearing structural pin is placement + direction: the gate is deny-only and runs BEFORE the I2 loop, so a PERMIT can never skip I2 and a permissive policy can never remove an I2 Block.

Output: the policy gate in the executor TCB (`policy_gate.rs` + a call site in `submit_plan_node`); the threaded param through brokerd/server; and the POLICY-01/POLICY-02 test suite. NO trusted-file binding yet (Plan 04) — `run_broker_server` constructs the policy value so the build stays green end-to-end.
</objective>
